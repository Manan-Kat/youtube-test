Life in Patiala.

Qila Mubarak

Quila Mubarak is the historical fort of the Patiala kingdom situated in the old city. It now houses a museum inside the Diwan-e-Aam. It is perfect for history enthusiasts as well as street food enthusiasts ;)

Sheesh Mahal

Sheesh Mahal is a gorgeous palace with magnificent floral decorations and mirror work. A massive suspension bridge connects the two sides of the palace. There is also a medal gallery inside. It is perfect for a calm Sunday picnic.

Baradari Gardens

The Baradari gardens are planted extensively with rare trees and shrubs, dotted with impressive Colonial buildings and a marble statue of Maharaja Rajindra Singh along with the Fern House. The 19th-century Fern House forms a unique attraction along with quaint Rink Hall.

Gurudwara dukhniwaran sahib

The historic Gurudwara is a pivotal part of the history of Patiala. The Sarovar is said to be blessed by Shri Guru Tegh Bahadur Ji and is believed to take away all sickness and suffering of any person who bathes in the holy waters. The aura of the place is healing and peaceful on its own.

Shri Kali Devi temple

The Maharaja Bhupinder Singh of Patiala built Kali Devi Mandir Shri Kali Devi Temple in 1936. The main temple hosts a 6-foot-tall statue of Maa Kali and the Paawan Jyot, which the Maharaja brought from Bengal himself. The temple complex houses various other smaller temples and has been declared a national monument.
